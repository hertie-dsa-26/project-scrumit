About Our Data

Real transactions. Real patterns. Real risk — without exposing real customer data.

The Dataset: We use IBM's synthetic AML dataset — over 5 million bank transactions engineered to mirror real-world money laundering behaviour. Because it's fully synthetic, there's zero privacy risk while still replicating the statistical fingerprints of genuine financial crime.

The ~0.1% laundering rate reflects real-world conditions — making detection genuinely hard. The HI-Small (High Illicit) variant gives us a slightly elevated ratio of suspicious transactions, which helps our model learn without drowning in negative examples.

Where It Comes From

1

IBM Research

IBM built this dataset using a multi-agent virtual world model that simulates realistic financial ecosystems — banks, accounts, and transaction flows — with laundering patterns injected synthetically. It's available publicly on Kaggle under the CDLA-Sharing-1.0 licence.

2

Why We Chose It

Most AML datasets are proprietary or anonymised beyond usefulness. IBM's gives us fully labelled data — every row tagged as laundering or legitimate — making it ideal for supervised Machine Learning. The HI-Small variant is sized for iterative development without sacrificing statistical richness.

What We Engineered on Top

Raw transaction fields alone aren't enough. Our pipeline adds derived signals that highlight suspicious patterns:

  • Currency Mismatch: Does the sending currency differ from the receiving currency?
  • Amount Ratio: How does the received amount compare to what was sent?
  • Geographic Distance: How far apart are the sending and receiving banks, computed via geopy?
  • Cross-entity Flags: Are funds moving between structurally unrelated entities?

How We Handle Scale

With 5+ million rows, running the full dataset on every iteration would be impractical. We use a 50,000 transaction development sample with stratified splits to keep runtimes fast without losing representativeness. The full dataset is reserved for final model training and evaluation.
